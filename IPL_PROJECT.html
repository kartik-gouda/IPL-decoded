Finding 01

Does winning the toss
actually matter?

Win % split by toss decision, across 1,095 matches. Chasing has a clear edge under lights.

Chose to field Chose to bat
A

Fielding first wins more often. Teams that bowled first after winning the toss took the match 53.4% of the time — likely dew and chase-friendly night pitches.

B

The gap widens at night. In matches starting after 7 PM, the fielding-first win rate climbs to 57.1%, reinforcing the dew-factor theory.

C

Captains have caught on. The share of captains electing to field first rose from 41% in 2008 to 78% in 2024.

Summary

Three takeaways for a match-day model

01

Bowl first when you can

Toss-winning captains should default to fielding, especially in night matches — the data backs a dew-driven chase advantage.

02

Death overs decide margins

Overs 17–20 contribute a disproportionate share of total runs; death-bowling economy is the single strongest predictor of a defended total.

03

Venue is a hidden feature

Ground dimensions swing average first-innings scores by 20+ runs — any win-predictor model should encode venue, not just teams.
